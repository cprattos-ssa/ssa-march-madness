Human In The Loop

The AI executes. The human steers.

Five moments where a question from the human changed the entire direction. Claude Code ran 12 autonomous agents - but these five sentences shaped what they built.

01
"Find non-obvious, data-driven insights - not generic bracket analysis."
Redirected from standard bracket breakdown to hunting for counterintuitive findings. Led to discovering the momentum myth (r=0.02), the 3-point paradox, and 40 years of unchanged upset rates.
02
"Add player-level data to the model. My team just lost - could individual player stats have predicted the upset?"
A real-world loss became a testable hypothesis. Triggered download of 30,066 player records via Barttorvik API, new feature module, full pipeline rebuild, and retraining across 30 configurations.
03
"Player data should help. Research why it didn't - check Kaggle discussions, academic papers, anything."
Refused to accept a counterintuitive result. Two parallel research agents searched Kaggle winning solutions, KenPom's noise analysis, and academic literature. Finding: no winning solution in the competition's 12-year history has ever used player data.
04
"Could we have used injury data pre-tournament? Were these absences public in time to adjust predictions?"
Pivoted from aggregate player analysis to actionable edge cases. Found all 8 major injuries were public 3-64 days before tip-off. Computed injury-adjusted predictions: Duke dropped from 99.4% to 86.6%.
05
"Limit the analysis window - structural breaks make pre-2020 comparisons misleading."
Identified that transfer portal, NIL, 3PT line move, and shot clock changes represent fundamentally different eras. Regenerated all timeline charts with structural break markers and created modern-era-only analytical figures.
