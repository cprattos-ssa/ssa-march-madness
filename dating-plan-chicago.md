# Chicago Dating Plan - Accountability Tracker

## Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-4)

### Define What You Want
- [ ] Write down top 5 non-negotiable values
- [ ] Identify 3-5 dealbreakers
- [ ] Clarify lifestyle preferences (kids, neighborhood, career priorities)

### Dating Profile Setup
- [ ] Set up Hinge profile
- [ ] Set up Bumble profile
- [ ] Get 5-6 quality photos (headshot, full-body, social, activity, lifestyle)
- [ ] Write specific prompts that invite conversation
- [ ] Have a trusted friend review your profile

### Weekly Routine
- [ ] Block 15 min/day for intentional app engagement
- [ ] Set a cap of 1-2 first dates per week

#### Chicago Options Menu
- **Sports leagues**: Chicago Sport & Social Club (CSSC) - volleyball, kickball, softball
- **Running clubs**: November Project (free, Lakefront), Fleet Feet run clubs
- **Climbing**: First Ascent gyms

- **Cultural**: Art Institute free evenings, Hideout, Empty Bottle, Green Mill jazz

### Build a "Third Place"
- [ ] Pick 1-2 coffee shops or bars to become a regular at
- [ ] Go at least 2x/week for the first month

## Phase 3: Dating with Intention (Ongoing)

### First Date Playbook
- Keep it 60-90 min, low-pressure
- Great Chicago first date spots:
  - Promontory (Hyde Park)
  - The Whistler (Logan Square)
  - Intelligentsia (multiple locations)
  - Walk through Lincoln Park Conservatory
- Ask real questions - skip "what do you do"

### Post-Date Reflection (fill out after each date)
- Did I feel energized or drained?
- Would I want to spend a boring Sunday with this person?
- Any red flags?
- Worth a second date? Why or why not?

### Second/Third Date Ideas
- Chicago Riverwalk walk
- Garfield Park Conservatory
- Neighborhood food crawl (Argyle Street, Pilsen)
- Museum of Contemporary Art

## Phase 4: Relationship Building

- [ ] Communicate intentions early (by date 3-5)

## Sustainability Rules

1. **Cap app time** - delete apps for a week if you feel cynical or burned out
2. **Keep living your life** - the best partners are attracted to people with full lives
3. **One new social thing per week** - treat it like a non-negotiable appointment
4. **Debrief with a friend** - external perspective prevents rationalizing red flags
5. **Therapy or coaching** - if you notice patterns, address them proactively

---

## Monthly Check-In Questions

At the end of each month, answer honestly:

1. Am I putting myself out there consistently, or have I gone passive?
2. Am I giving people a fair chance (2-3 dates), or writing them off too quickly?
3. Am I repeating any patterns from past relationships?
4. What's one thing I can do differently next month?
5. Am I still enjoying the process, or do I need a reset?
