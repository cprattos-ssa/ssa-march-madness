Discovery
Features that hurt the model are exactly what flagged the upsets
Team WAR predicted the upset winner in 7 of 12 wrong picks. The aggregate doesn't need them. The edge cases desperately do.

Validated Findings
WAR flags edge cases
Team WAR correctly identified the upset winner in the majority of the model's catastrophic misses, despite being useless in aggregate training.
7 / 12 wrong picks flagged
Injuries are invisible signal
8 players out pre-tipoff. Duke's prediction dropped 13 points when adjusted. Gonzaga missing Huff (17.8 PPG). BYU missing Saunders (18.0 PPG).
Public 3-64 days before tipoff
Probability clipping needed
Model output 97.1% and 96.1% raw. Past Kaggle winners clip to [2.5%, 97.5%]. Would have saved 0.15+ Brier on worst misses alone.
No clipping applied
Elo dominates everything
Elo_diff is the #1 feature. When Elo disagrees with WAR, Sr%, coaching experience, Elo always wins. Creates extreme confidence in inflated ratings.
12/12 misses: Elo favored our pick
No player data in winners
No documented Kaggle March Madness winner has used player-level features. goto_conversion ($47K in prizes) uses zero ML.
Confirmed via competition history
Conclusion: Player data is noise in aggregate, signal at the edges, and critical for injury adjustment.
